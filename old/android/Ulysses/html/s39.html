“We've got her now!” Security Chief Blake Thomas said, grinning from behind his security console.

The three loyal Federation starships moved in on the Sunriser. It was facing away from the Ulysses, and had yet to show any sign that it was aware of the three Federation starships.

“Ulysses to fleet: Don't fire until I give the command, we can't chance them getting away! Vontarres, move to block their path, if they should try to escape,” the captain ordered.

“Five thousand spatials,” Ensign Hob counted off the distance to their prey.

“Four-point-five thousand spatials,” he continued as minutes slowly ticked by, and the three ships drew closer to the enemy.

“Hold us steady, Lieutenant Lewis,” the captain ordered.

Suddenly an energy stream lashed out from the cruiser following the Ulysses, blasting through the cloud toward the Sunriser, but at that range, it missed completely!

“Damn-it!” the captain cursed the trigger-happy helmsman of the other ship. The Sunriser, quite aware now of their presence, began to move forwards at a steadily increasing rate.

As the Sunriser began to move out of the Ulysses' weapons range, it moved right into the Vontarres' line of fire. The Sunriser changed its heading, and fired streams of energy at the ship in its path!

Three artificial lightning bolts arced out from the Cassian cruiser, but with the nebula's effect on sensors and targeting systems, two of the energy streams flew wide of their target. The other struck the side of the Vontarres, blasting across the unshielded hull on its port side, sending red-hot fragments of plasteel scattering into the cloud.

The three Federation starships closed in, and in a concerted effort, multiple streams of artificial lightning lashed out from their ships, arcing through the nebula cloud towards the Sunriser...

Only two of the energy bolts hit their target, but without its energy shields, the Sunriser took a harsh beating. Hull plating blasted away into space, glowing red from the superheated energy that bored into the ship.

Captain Donaldson leaned back into his seat, the tension of the moment beginning to fade, as he saw the Sunriser tilt to one side, and begin to drift aimlessly.

“We've got her!” Lieutenant Lewis shouted, to a brief cheer from the other officers.

“Steady now,” Captain Donaldson said. “Our task is not yet completed. We need prisoners – High Command has questions they want answered.”
